MINEFLEX

BETA LANDING PAGE

Mineflex is a Minecraft Server Hosting platform that operates on a cloud-computing model

After months of discussions, research and back-end engineering, Mineflex inched closer to a beta launch. The last, essential piece Mineflex needed was a landing page. So, in August I learned HTML/CSS and began my design journey through the development of the the Beta Landing Page.

The landing page evolved heavily from its first design to the one that was launched. The final design goal aims to present our product through clear messaging while using customer pain-points to result in a conversion. Choosing a few key elements like the large header, descriptive sub-head and CTA box maintained a clean look and feel while focusing on one central message. The details below the head were arranged in the order we felt most relevant to potential users.

THE CHALLENGE

Our product was still in the early stages and we needed to find one central message to communicate with potenial beta users.

The landing page would be the first introduction not just to Mineflex, but also a new way of Minecraft server hosting. The biggest challenge was maintaining a strong dedication to the effective landing page "formula," while trying to communicate that our server hosting was a fundamentally different product than traditional MC Server Hosts.

OUR APPROACH

The current landing page grew out of many dicussions about what our main selling point should be. We continuously asked questions:

What is the most important aspect of our service for a potential user?

How do we create a narrative around our product?

And how do we center that narrative around user needs while still communicating essential information?

THE DETAILS

Overview

The landing page design was a big step in my design process. It was my first project that I designed and developed on my own. I was unhappy with my original design which led me to hone in on Mineflex's central purpose and highlight key features that communicated that purpose.

Full Beta Landing Page

Key Features

The first images below are from my first landing page design. Although not the final product, the early designs taught me important lessons that that led me to create a successful landing page for the beta launch.

Although I like this design it does not accomplish what we needed from a landing page. The title and sub-title descriptions present too much information and the lack of a distinct CTA box did not perform well when we A/B tested against our later version.

The redesign highlighted hourly billing, free beta signups and the Mineflex Discord. By using a large catchy headline, CTA box and feature color to emphasize the free beta I was able to combine multiple messages into one coherent page without overwhelming a potential visitor.

We chose to highlight certain features based on market research. Early research had revealed Minecraft players' frustration with poorly designed control panels and extra costs for features they felt should be included.

1. Leveraging Patterns

When I first began the Mineflex Landing page design I focused too much of my energy on trying to come up with a design that was different than the designs of landing pages I liked. Taking a step back from my initial designs and finding a few common denomenators helped me look for patterns that I could mimic and making them unique by putting them in a context that made sense for Mineflex. Out of that grew a landing page that was both effective and unique.

2. Focus on Performance

Implementing Google Analytics allowed us to see that the original landing page design was not performing in the way we had hoped for. By focusing on concrete metrics like bounce rate and conversions we were able to realize quickly that we needed to rethink and focus-in on one message and tweak the design to highlight only essential information. Although this meant additional work in the short term, tracking the landing page performance meant we were able to pivot and set a new course saving us time in the long term.

3. Present a Well-Rounded Product

Portions of the Mineflex Landing Page required a lot of technical knowledge. Aspects like DDoS protection, TCP load balanced clusters and network edge mitigation were important aspects of Mineflex. Sitting down with the product engineer and discussing the technical details of the product helped us collaborate on the copy, resulting in a well-rounded landing page, not devoid of technical information that was important for some beta users.
