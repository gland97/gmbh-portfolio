MINEFLEX

Server Creation Process

Mineflex is a Minecraft Server Hosting platform that operates on a cloud-computing model

After months of discussions, research and back-end engineering, Mineflex inched closer to a beta launch. But we needed one more important piece â€“ a landing page.

The landing page evolved heavily from its first design to the one that was launched. The final design goal aims to present our product through clear messaging while using customer pain-points to result in a conversion. Choosing a few key elements like the large header, descriptive sub-head and CTA box maintained a clean look and feel while focusing on one central message. The details below the head were arranged in the order we felt most relevant to potential users.

THE CHALLENGE

Our product was still in the early stages and we needed to find one central message we wanted to communicate with potenial beta users.

The landing page would be the first introduction not just to Mineflex, but also a new way of Minecraft server hosting. The biggest challenge was maintaining a strong dedication to the effective landing page "formula," while trying to communicate that our server hosting was a fundamentally different product than traditional MC Server Hosts.

OUR APPROACH

The current landing page grew out of many dicussions about what our main selling point should be. We continuously asked questions:

What is the most important aspect of our service for a potential user?

How do we create a narrative around our product?

And how do we center that narrative around user needs while still comunicating essential information?

LANDING PAGE INSPIRATION

After a few designs that were not headed in the right direction, I went back to the basics and found design inspiration from clean, to-the-point websites like Digital Ocean and Discord.
